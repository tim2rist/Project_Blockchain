Introduction

Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across many computers in such a way that the records cannot be altered retroactively.

How It Works

The blockchain operates by grouping transactions into blocks. These blocks are then validated by a network of computers using consensus mechanisms like proof-of-work or proof-of-stake.

Applications

Blockchain technology has applications in various fields, including finance (cryptocurrencies), supply chain management, healthcare, and voting systems.

Future Prospects

The future of blockchain technology looks promising, with advancements expected in scalability, energy efficiency, and broader adoption in industries worldwide.
